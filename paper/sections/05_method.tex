\section{The PA-DCT Policy}
\label{sec:method}

PA-DCT instantiates the policy side of the 402Pilot layer. Its job is narrow: inside \texttt{select}, choose one affordable provider; inside \texttt{update}, learn only from the chosen outcome. The policy keeps two beliefs for each provider--task bucket, one about service utility and one about realized cost. At selection time it discounts old evidence, samples both beliefs, combines them with the current wallet pressure, and returns the provider with the best sampled trade-off. After the chosen call, it receives $(\ut,c_t)$ and updates only the chosen cell. The payment-aware reward $\rt$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:reward}) is used for decision-time scoring and benchmark evaluation; provider beliefs are learned from $(\ut,c_t)$, never from $\rt$.

\subsection{Utility and Cost Beliefs}
\label{sec:method-posteriors}

Let $k_t=k(\mathbf{x}_t)$ map a task context to one of $K_b$ buckets; in the benchmark $K_b=4$, one bucket for each task type in \S\ref{sec:eval-setup}. For every provider (arm) $a$ and bucket $k$, PA-DCT maintains:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Q-posterior}: a Normal--Normal posterior over utility $\ut=q_t-\nu f_t$.
\item \textbf{C-posterior}: a Normal--Normal posterior over realized cost $c_t$.
\end{itemize}

Each cell stores an effective count $n^{a,k}_t$ shared by the two posteriors (each pull contributes one utility observation and one cost observation), a utility sum $S^{a,k}_t$, and a cost sum ${S_c}^{a,k}_t$. The Q-posterior has prior $\mathcal{N}(\mu_{0,q},\sigma^2_{0,q})$ and likelihood variance $\sigma^2_q$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:posterior-q}
\begin{aligned}
\Sigmaq{a}{k}
&= \left(\frac{1}{\sigma^2_{0,q}}
  + \frac{n^{a,k}_t}{\sigma^2_q}\right)^{-1},\\
\thetaq{a}{k}
&= \Sigmaq{a}{k}
  \left(\frac{\mu_{0,q}}{\sigma^2_{0,q}}
  + \frac{S^{a,k}_t}{\sigma^2_q}\right).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The C-posterior uses the same form with $(\thetac{a}{k},\Sigmac{a}{k})$, prior mean $\bar c_a$ (the arm's spec price), likelihood variance $\sigma^2_c$, and cost sum ${S_c}^{a,k}_t$.

The learned C-posterior is what lets PA-DCT respond to price drift. If prices were fixed and visible before selection, the spec price $\bar c_a$ would be enough. In the 402Pilot contract, however, the policy sees the affordable set before payment and learns numeric charges only from receipts. PA-DCT therefore treats $\bar c_a$ as a prior and lets selected charges $c_t=p_{a_t,t}$ move the cost belief, just as selected utilities move the Q-posterior.

\subsection{Discounted Updates}
\label{sec:method-discount}

To track non-stationarity, PA-DCT discounts every cell at the start of each round:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
n^{a,k}_t &= \gamma n^{a,k}_{t-1},\\
S^{a,k}_t &= \gamma S^{a,k}_{t-1},\\
{S_c}^{a,k}_t &= \gamma {S_c}^{a,k}_{t-1}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
After the chosen provider returns feedback, only the chosen cell receives the new observation:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
n^{a_t,k_t}_t &\mathrel{+}= 1,\\
S^{a_t,k_t}_t &\mathrel{+}= \ut,\\
{S_c}^{a_t,k_t}_t &\mathrel{+}= c_t.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Discounting is round-based rather than pull-conditional: every cell ages once per round, because a provider can become better, worse, cheaper, or more expensive even while it is not selected. We use $\gamma=0.999$ for both posteriors, giving an effective horizon near $10^3$ rounds and a half-life of about $693$ rounds; Appendix~\ref{appendix:sensitivity} discusses this choice.

\subsection{Selection Rule}
\label{sec:method-decision}

At round $t$, PA-DCT receives $\mathbf{x}_t$ and the affordable set $\mathcal{A}_t$ from the layer, and reads wallet pressure $\lambda_t$ through the optional channel defined in \S\ref{sec:layer}. It sets $\lamn=\lambda_t/(1+\lambda_t)$ and draws one Thompson sample from each posterior for every affordable arm:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:padct-sample}
\begin{aligned}
\hatu{a}{k_t}
&\sim \mathcal{N}\!\big(\thetaq{a}{k_t},\, \Sigmaq{a}{k_t}\big),\\
\hatc{a}{k_t}
&\sim \mathcal{N}\!\big(\thetac{a}{k_t},\, \Sigmac{a}{k_t}\big).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The sampled score mirrors the payment-aware reward, using sampled utility and sampled normalized cost:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:padct-score}
\begin{aligned}
\tilde{\hat c}^{a,k_t}
&= \mathrm{clip}\!\big(\hatc{a}{k_t}/c_{\max},\,0,\,1\big),\\
s_a
&= (1-\lamn)\,\hatu{a}{k_t}
  - \lamn\,\tilde{\hat c}^{a,k_t}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The policy chooses $a_t=\arg\max_{a\in\mathcal{A}_t}s_a$, with deterministic tie-breaking. Algorithm~\ref{alg:padct} states the round loop in the \texttt{select}/\texttt{update} contract; if $\mathcal{A}_t$ is empty, the layer stops the episode before invoking the policy.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{PA-DCT policy loop.}
\label{alg:padct}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Spec costs $\bar c_a$; discount $\gamma$; normalizer $c_{\max}$.
\Require Encoder $k(\cdot)$ and Q/C posterior priors.
\For{$(a,k)\in\{1,\ldots,K\}\times\{1,\ldots,K_b\}$}
  \State $n^{a,k}\leftarrow 0$;\quad $S^{a,k}\leftarrow 0$;\quad ${S_c}^{a,k}\leftarrow 0$
\EndFor
\For{each served round $t$ with $\mathcal{A}_t\neq\emptyset$}
  \State Receive $\mathbf{x}_t$ and nonempty $\mathcal{A}_t$ from the layer
  \State For all $(a,k)$: discount $n^{a,k}$, $S^{a,k}$, and ${S_c}^{a,k}$ by $\gamma$
  \State Read $\lambda_t$;\quad $k_t\leftarrow k(\mathbf{x}_t)$
  \State $\lamn\leftarrow\lambda_t/(1+\lambda_t)$
  \For{$a\in\mathcal{A}_t$}
    \State Sample $\hatu{a}{k_t}$ and $\hatc{a}{k_t}$ using Eq.~\eqref{eq:padct-sample}
    \State $s_a\leftarrow(1-\lamn)\hatu{a}{k_t}-\lamn\,\mathrm{clip}(\hatc{a}{k_t}/c_{\max},0,1)$
  \EndFor
  \State Select $a_t\leftarrow\arg\max_{a\in\mathcal{A}_t}s_a$
  \State Receive update feedback $(\ut,c_t)$ from the layer
  \State $n^{a_t,k_t}\mathrel{+}=1$;\quad $S^{a_t,k_t}\mathrel{+}=\ut$;\quad ${S_c}^{a_t,k_t}\mathrel{+}=c_t$
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Locked Settings}
\label{sec:method-hyperparams}

All experiments use one locked configuration. For the Q-posterior, we set $\mu_{0,q}=0.5$, $\sigma^2_{0,q}=1.0$, and $\sigma^2_q=0.09$. For the C-posterior, we set prior mean $\bar c_a$, $\sigma^2_{0,c}=10^{-4}$, and $\sigma^2_c=10^{-6}$. The remaining constants are $\gamma=0.999$, $\nu=0.5$, $\alpha=2.0$ in the wallet-pressure schedule, and $c_{\max}=\$0.01$, the highest listed provider price. The default bucket map uses task type only ($K_b=4$), while budget pressure enters through $\lambda_t$ rather than through the bucket index. These values are fixed before evaluation and are not tuned per scenario or seed; Appendix~\ref{appendix:sensitivity} records the sensitivity evidence and unswept constants.

\subsection{Scope and Computation}
\label{sec:method-theory}

PA-DCT combines standard ingredients: contextual Thompson sampling~\citep{russo2014learning}, discounted sufficient statistics for non-stationary bandits~\citep{garivier2011discounted,qi2023dsts}, and budget-aware scoring in the spirit of bandits with knapsacks~\citep{badanidiyuru2018bandits}. Appendix~\ref{appendix:proofs} states the formal scope. In brief, the payment-aware reward is bounded, and per-cell posteriors are consistent in the stationary no-discount limit. Under stationary, well-specified, no-discount assumptions, PA-DCT reduces to per-bucket Gaussian Thompson sampling and inherits an $\tilde O(\sqrt{K K_b T})$ Bayesian PA-regret bound. With discounting, old evidence after a change point is forgotten at rate $\gamma^\tau$, yielding the recovery horizon in Cor.~\ref{cor:recovery}. This is not a full policy-level regret theorem for every piecewise-stationary wallet trajectory; Appendix~\ref{app:proof-limitations} records the remaining boundaries.

The per-round policy cost is $O(KK_b+|\mathcal{A}_t|)$ time and $O(KK_b)$ memory: a discount sweep over all cells, two Gaussian samples for each affordable arm, and a constant-time update of the chosen cell. In our experiments $K=5$ and $K_b=4$, so the policy maintains twenty cells and the time cost is $O(KK_b)$. For larger provider sets, the discount sweep can be implemented lazily with per-cell timestamps. This bound is only the policy-computation cost; 402Pilot-Bench removes live model-inference and settlement latency so the experiments measure decision quality rather than deployment latency.
