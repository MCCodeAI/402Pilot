\section{Method: PA-DCT}
\label{sec:method}

PA-DCT is an instance of contextual Thompson sampling adapted to the agent-native payment decision-making setting (\S\ref{sec:intro}). Each (arm, task-type-bucket) cell maintains \emph{two} independent Gaussian posteriors---one over utility, one over cost---both tracked through discounted sufficient statistics. The scalar reward $\rt$ in Eq.~\eqref{eq:reward} is therefore a ranking and evaluation objective, not a posterior target. At decision time, the policy samples both posteriors and ranks affordable arms by a budget-pressure-weighted score; on receipt of the realized outcome, only the chosen cell's posteriors are updated, after a global discount sweep over every cell.

\subsection{Dual Gaussian posteriors}
\label{sec:method-posteriors}

For each arm $a \in \{1,\ldots,K\}$ and bucket $k \in \{1,\ldots,K_b\}$ (where $K_b$ is the number of context buckets, default $4$, one per evaluation bucket / task type), we maintain two posteriors:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Q-posterior}, tracking the per-arm utility $\ut = q_t - \nu f_t$. Conjugate Normal-Normal with prior $\mathcal{N}(\mu_{0,q}, \sigma^2_{0,q})$ and likelihood variance $\sigma^2_q$.
\item \textbf{C-posterior}, tracking the per-arm raw cost $c_t$. Same Normal-Normal form, with prior $\mathcal{N}(\bar c_a, \sigma^2_{0,c})$ where $\bar c_a$ is the spec base price for arm $a$, and likelihood variance $\sigma^2_c$.
\end{itemize}

Each cell carries discounted sufficient statistics: a shared effective sample count $n^{a,k}_t \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$, a weighted sum of utilities $S^{a,k}_t \in \mathbb{R}$ for the Q-posterior, and a weighted sum of realized costs ${S_c}^{a,k}_t \in \mathbb{R}$ for the C-posterior. Posterior parameters follow the closed-form Normal--Normal expressions, e.g.\ for Q:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:posterior-q}
\Sigmaq{a}{k} = \left(\frac{1}{\sigma^2_{0,q}} + \frac{n^{a,k}_t}{\sigma^2_q}\right)^{-1}, \qquad
\thetaq{a}{k} = \Sigmaq{a}{k} \left(\frac{\mu_{0,q}}{\sigma^2_{0,q}} + \frac{S^{a,k}_t}{\sigma^2_q}\right).
\end{equation}
The C-posterior uses the analogous form with $(\thetac{a}{k}, \Sigmac{a}{k})$, $\bar c_a$ in place of $\mu_{0,q}$, $\sigma^2_c$ in place of $\sigma^2_q$, and ${S_c}^{a,k}_t$ in place of $S^{a,k}_t$.

\paragraph{Why a learned cost posterior.}
If realized prices were fixed and observed before selection, PA-DCT could use the spec price $\bar c_a$ directly. Our decision-layer contract deliberately does not assume this: effective prices $p_{a,t}$ may drift over time, and the policy observes only the feasible set $\mathcal{A}_t$ before payment, not the full price vector $\mathbf{p}_t$. The spec price $\bar c_a$ therefore serves as the C-posterior prior mean, while selected calls reveal realized charges $c_t=p_{a_t,t}$. Learning a posterior over $c_t$ lets PA-DCT react to price shocks through the same discounted evidence mechanism used by the Q-posterior for quality and reliability shocks.

\subsection{Discounted sufficient statistics}
\label{sec:method-discount}

We apply a multiplicative discount $\gamma \in (0, 1]$ to every cell's sufficient statistics at the start of each round, before any new observation arrives:
\begin{equation}
n^{a,k}_t = \gamma \cdot n^{a,k}_{t-1}, \qquad
S^{a,k}_t = \gamma \cdot S^{a,k}_{t-1}, \qquad
{S_c}^{a,k}_t = \gamma \cdot {S_c}^{a,k}_{t-1}.
\end{equation}
After the round's chosen arm produces an observation, only its bucket increments:
\begin{equation}
n^{a_t, k_t}_t \mathrel{+}= 1, \quad
S^{a_t, k_t}_t \mathrel{+}= \ut\ \,(\text{Q})\ , \quad
{S_c}^{a_t, k_t}_t \mathrel{+}= c_t\ \,(\text{C}).
\end{equation}
Discounting applies regardless of whether a cell was pulled this round---non-stationarity is wall-clock, not pull-conditional. We use $\gamma = 0.999$ for both posteriors (effective half-life $\approx 693$ rounds), chosen to remain responsive within shock windows of a few thousand rounds without forgetting the stationary baseline too quickly. The role of $\gamma$ in controlling post-shock adaptation speed is analyzed in Cor.~\ref{cor:recovery}.

\subsection{Decision rule}
\label{sec:method-decision}

At round $t$, after observing $\mathbf{x}_t$ and setting $k_t=k(\mathbf{x}_t)$, the wallet/payment interface provides the budget-pressure multiplier $\lambda_t$ and the affordable set $\mathcal{A}_t$. PA-DCT sets $\lamn=\lambda_t/(1+\lambda_t)$ and scores each affordable arm using independent Thompson samples from the Q- and C-posteriors for the current bucket:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:padct-sample}
\hatu{a}{k_t} \sim \mathcal{N}\!\big(\thetaq{a}{k_t},\, \Sigmaq{a}{k_t}\big), \qquad
\hatc{a}{k_t} \sim \mathcal{N}\!\big(\thetac{a}{k_t},\, \Sigmac{a}{k_t}\big),
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:padct-score}
\tilde{\hat c}^{a,k_t} = \mathrm{clip}\!\big(\hatc{a}{k_t}/c_{\max},\, 0,\, 1\big), \qquad
s_a = (1 - \lamn)\,\hatu{a}{k_t} - \lamn\,\tilde{\hat c}^{a,k_t}.
\end{equation}
The chosen arm is $a_t = \arg\max_{a \in \mathcal{A}_t} s_a$, with ties broken by any fixed rule. We write $s_a$ for the per-arm decision score to avoid clashing with the policy symbol $\pi$. Algorithm~\ref{alg:padct} states the per-round procedure end-to-end.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{PA-DCT: Payment-Aware Discounted Contextual Thompson sampling.}
\label{alg:padct}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Provider set $\{1,\ldots,K\}$ with spec costs $\bar c_a$; discount $\gamma$; failure penalty $\nu$; cost normalizer $c_{\max}$; context encoder $k(\cdot)$; Q-posterior priors $(\mu_{0,q},\sigma^2_{0,q},\sigma^2_q)$; C-posterior priors $(\sigma^2_{0,c},\sigma^2_c)$; wallet/payment interface returning $\lambda_t$ and the affordable set $\mathcal{A}_t$.
\For{$(a, k) \in \{1,\ldots,K\} \times \{1,\ldots,K_b\}$}
  \State $n^{a,k} \leftarrow 0$;\quad $S^{a,k} \leftarrow 0$;\quad ${S_c}^{a,k} \leftarrow 0$
\EndFor
\For{$t = 0, 1, \ldots, T-1$}
  \State For all $(a,k)$: $n^{a,k} \leftarrow \gamma\,n^{a,k}$;\ $S^{a,k} \leftarrow \gamma\,S^{a,k}$;\ ${S_c}^{a,k} \leftarrow \gamma\,{S_c}^{a,k}$
  \State Observe $\mathbf{x}_t$, $B_t$;\quad $k_t \leftarrow k(\mathbf{x}_t)$
  \State Read $\lambda_t$ and $\mathcal{A}_t$ from the wallet/payment interface;\quad $\lamn \leftarrow \lambda_t / (1 + \lambda_t)$
  \If{$\mathcal{A}_t = \emptyset$} terminate the episode (no affordable provider) \EndIf
  \For{$a \in \mathcal{A}_t$}
    \State $\hatu{a}{k_t} \sim \mathcal{N}\!\big(\thetaq{a}{k_t},\,\Sigmaq{a}{k_t}\big)$;\quad $\hatc{a}{k_t} \sim \mathcal{N}\!\big(\thetac{a}{k_t},\,\Sigmac{a}{k_t}\big)$
    \State $\tilde{\hat c}^{a,k_t} \leftarrow \mathrm{clip}(\hatc{a}{k_t}/c_{\max}, 0, 1)$;\quad $s_a \leftarrow (1-\lamn)\,\hatu{a}{k_t} - \lamn\,\tilde{\hat c}^{a,k_t}$
  \EndFor
  \State $a_t \leftarrow \arg\max_{a \in \mathcal{A}_t} s_a$
  \State Pay via the protocol; observe $(q_t, c_t, f_t)$;\quad $\ut \leftarrow q_t - \nu f_t$
  \State Update chosen cell: $n^{a_t,k_t} \mathrel{+}= 1$;\quad $S^{a_t,k_t} \mathrel{+}= \ut$;\quad ${S_c}^{a_t,k_t} \mathrel{+}= c_t$
  \State wallet.\texttt{record\_spend}$(c_t)$
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Hyperparameters}
\label{sec:method-hyperparams}

We fix the following constants across every experiment in the paper:
\begin{itemize}
\item Quality posterior: $\mu_{0,q} = 0.5$, $\sigma^2_{0,q} = 1.0$, $\sigma^2_q = 0.09$ (allowing substantial heterogeneity in observed quality).
\item Cost posterior: $\sigma^2_{0,c} = 10^{-4}$, $\sigma^2_c = 10^{-6}$ (tight, because realized cost is nearly deterministic given the provider and scenario).
\item Discounts: $\gamma_q = \gamma_c = 0.999$.
\item Reward weights and bandit constants: $\nu = 0.5$, $\alpha = 2$, $c_{\max} = \$0.01$.
\item Bucket count: $K_b = 4$ (one per task type, see \S\ref{sec:eval-setup}).
\end{itemize}
None of these are tuned per scenario or per seed.

\subsection{Theoretical properties}
\label{sec:method-theory}

PA-DCT is a bandit-family method. Its constituent components---contextual Thompson sampling~\citep{russo2014learning}, discounted sufficient statistics for non-stationary bandits~\citep{garivier2011discounted}, and budget-aware reward shaping in the spirit of bandits-with-knapsacks~\citep{badanidiyuru2018bandits}---are each individually well-studied. Our analysis confirms that the specific combination instantiated by PA-DCT, which to our knowledge has not been analyzed jointly before in the agent-economy regime, preserves the standard properties expected of its parts.

Specifically, in Appendix~\ref{appendix:proofs} we establish four properties: (i) the payment-aware reward is bounded, $\rt \in [-1, +1]$ (Prop.~\ref{prop:reward-bounded}); (ii) per-cell posteriors are almost-surely consistent in the stationary regime (Prop.~\ref{prop:consistency}); (iii) under a well-specified Gaussian Bayesian model and $\gamma=1$, the PA-DCT decision rule reduces to per-bucket contextual Gaussian-TS, yielding an $\tilde O(\sqrt{K K_b T})$ Bayesian regret bound by reduction to \citet{russo2014learning} Theorem~1 (Thm.~\ref{thm:stationary-regret}, under the assumptions detailed in App.~\ref{appendix:proofs}); and (iv) after an abrupt change-point of magnitude $\Delta$, the per-arm Q-posterior bias admits an exact closed-form bound, yielding a recovery horizon $\tau \geq \log(\Delta/\delta)/\log(1/\gamma)$ for any target tolerance $\delta$ in the data-dominated regime (Thm.~\ref{thm:adaptation}, Cor.~\ref{cor:recovery}). The discount factor $\gamma$ thus controls an explicit per-arm responsiveness to drift; for $\gamma = 0.999$ the effective horizon is roughly $1{,}000$ rounds (Lemma~\ref{lem:ess}).

We do not establish a coupled regret bound for piecewise-stationary regimes with budget pressure, nor a misspecified-likelihood version of Thm.~\ref{thm:stationary-regret}; full theorem statements, proofs, and limitations of this analysis are deferred to Appendix~\ref{appendix:proofs} (limitations in App.~\ref{app:proof-limitations}).

\subsection{Computational complexity}
\label{sec:method-complexity}

Per round, PA-DCT performs (i) a global discount sweep over every cell, $O(K K_b)$, (ii) two Gaussian samples and one $\arg\max$ over the affordable set $\mathcal{A}_t$, $O(|\mathcal{A}_t|) \leq O(K)$, and (iii) one closed-form Normal--Normal update on the chosen cell, $O(1)$. Total per-round cost is $O(K K_b)$ time and $O(K K_b)$ memory; with the locked $K = 5$ and $K_b = 4$ this is twenty cells. The discount sweep is the only operation whose cost grows with the policy's state, and is trivially parallelizable across cells. End-to-end wall-clock numbers and the LLM-replay setup that makes them small are reported in \S\ref{sec:eval-setup}.
