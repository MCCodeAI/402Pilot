\section{The 402Pilot Decision Layer}
\label{sec:layer}

402Pilot is a decision layer between an autonomous agent and a programmable payment protocol such as x402. The layer accepts a task and wallet state, outputs a provider choice, asks the protocol stack to execute the selected payment, and feeds the realized outcome back into a policy. It does not modify the protocol, never holds funds, and never crosses the settlement boundary. Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} sketches the per-round flow.

\begin{figure*}[t]
\centering

\caption{The 402Pilot decision-layer architecture. The layer encodes the task context, restricts selection to wallet-feasible providers, and calls \texttt{policy.select}; settlement and service execution remain outside the layer. After the provider responds, the outcome scorer and payment receipt produce $(q_t,c_t,f_t)$ for \texttt{policy.update}.}
\label{fig:architecture}
\end{figure*}

The architectural contract is intentionally small. A policy is any object exposing
\begin{align*}
&\mathtt{select}(\mathbf{x}_t,\, \mathcal{A}_t) \to a_t \in \mathcal{A}_t, \\
&\mathtt{update}(\mathbf{x}_t,\, a_t,\, \ut,\, c_t) \to \emptyset,
\end{align*}
where $\mathcal{A}_t \subseteq \{1, \ldots, K\}$ is the wallet-feasible subset of providers at round $t$. The candidate provider set is supplied by an external discovery or registry component; the wallet/payment interface evaluates feasibility against the round's effective prices $p_{a,t}$ and passes only the resulting feasible set into the policy. Numeric effective charges are not passed to the policy pre-payment. Wallet pressure is an optional dependency: PA-DCT reads $\lambda_t$ from the wallet inside \texttt{select}, while non-payment-aware comparators carry no wallet dependency. The two methods are therefore the only points of contact with the rest of the stack; priors, posteriors, and other internal state remain private to the policy.

A key design choice is that the layer feeds \emph{utility} $\ut$ to \texttt{policy.update}, rather than the payment-aware reward $\rt$. The same payment-aware form defines the decision-time ranking score and the evaluation objective, but realized $\rt$ should not be treated as an observation of provider quality. Updating posteriors on $\rt$ would make the same provider appear worse late in a budget-tight run than early in the run, even if its service behavior had not changed. In 402Pilot, beliefs about provider utility and cost are updated from $(\ut,c_t)$, while wallet pressure enters selection through $\lambda_t$.

This boundary is payment-rail agnostic. x402 is one current instantiation, but the substrate is unsettled---traditional payment rails may evolve to expose similar pay-per-call interfaces---and the same contract can sit above any rail that lists prices, executes the selected payment, and returns a receipt. The intelligence an agent needs at this boundary belongs in the decision layer rather than the payment stack: settlement, provider discovery, observability, multi-agent coordination, and strategic bidding remain outside the layer.
