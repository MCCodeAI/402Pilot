\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Programmable settlement makes it possible for agents to pay, but it does not decide how an agent should spend a finite wallet. This paper formalized that missing problem as \emph{agent-native payment decision-making}: buyer-side provider selection under uncertain quality, changing reliability or prices, task context, and budget pressure. 402Pilot separates this decision from payment execution, and PA-DCT gives a concrete policy that learns from both service outcomes and realized costs.

On 402Pilot-Bench, PA-DCT trades stationary-regime performance for adaptation capacity: it remains budget-feasible while improving service quality during an outage and captures a price-promotion opportunity without provider-specific rules. The component results explain why. Payment-aware ranking keeps the wallet solvent by making cost matter more as burn rate rises; discounting lets stale quality and cost evidence fade after market changes; the learned cost posterior lets the policy respond to realized prices rather than only listed rates; and contextual bucketing with Thompson sampling avoids brittle one-arm collapse while preserving task-specific exploration. Ablations and the targeted sensitivity sweep support the same interpretation: the policy's behavior is governed by the intended design levers rather than by scenario-specific parameter choices.

Across the evaluation panel, PA-DCT is the only non-oracle policy that remains jointly competitive in service quality, spending efficiency, and payment-aware objective across all three market regimes, supporting a single adaptive buyer policy rather than scenario-specific rules. The broader conclusion is that autonomous payment systems need learning and evaluation at the buyer layer, not only safer settlement mechanisms. We release the code, frozen replay data, scenario and sensitivity sweeps, evaluation harness, and a local x402 integration witness at \href{https://github.com/MCCodeAI/402Pilot}{\texttt{MCCodeAI/402Pilot}} to make this decision layer testable and extensible.
