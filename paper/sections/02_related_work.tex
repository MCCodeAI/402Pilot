\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}

\paragraph{Programmable payment protocols and discovery.}
Recent agent-payment protocols make it possible for software agents to pay for online services at machine speed. x402 embeds per-request payment into HTTP, and its V2 release adds automatic discovery, dynamic payment recipients, extensible payment schemes, and dynamic service metadata, including pricing information~\citep{coinbase2025x402v2}. AP2 provides a payment-agnostic authorization framework for agent-led commerce~\citep{google2025ap2}; A2A--x402 brings x402-style cryptocurrency payments into the Agent-to-Agent protocol~\citep{a2ax402}; and A402 binds cryptocurrency payment to service execution through atomic service channels~\citep{a402}. A recent SoK further organizes blockchain-based agent payments around discovery, authorization, execution, and accounting~\citep{sok2026agentpayments}. These works specify the substrate for making payable services visible and executable. They do not specify the buyer-side policy that ranks discovered endpoints, spends a finite wallet, and updates provider choice from realized prices, failures, and service outcomes. 402Pilot is therefore complementary to protocol work: it sits above payment execution and decides which payable endpoint to use.

\paragraph{Budgeted, contextual, and non-stationary bandits.}
Classical bandit work supplies the main algorithmic ingredients for our policy. Budgeted Thompson Sampling samples reward and cost posteriors under a finite budget~\citep{xia2015bts}, while bandits with knapsacks give a broader constrained-resource formulation~\citep{badanidiyuru2018bandits}. Contextual bandits estimate action value from task features, with Contextual Thompson Sampling and LinUCB as canonical examples~\citep{agrawal2013contextualts,li2010linucb}. Contextual bandits with knapsacks combine context with resource constraints~\citep{agrawal2016linearcbwk}. For non-stationary environments, discounted and sliding-window methods down-weight stale observations~\citep{garivier2011discounted,trovo2020sliding,qi2023dsts}, and weighted linear bandits extend this idea to drifting contextual rewards~\citep{russac2019weighted}. These results cover important subsets of our design space, but they are not formulated as buyer-side policies inside a protocol-mediated quote--pay--observe loop. In particular, they do not jointly address a draining wallet, task-conditioned provider quality, non-stationary quality and price, and realized payment cost as feedback in autonomous agent micropayments.

\paragraph{Cost-aware LLM routing.}
A growing literature studies quality--cost tradeoffs in LLM routing. FrugalGPT uses cascades to reduce cost while preserving answer quality~\citep{chen2023frugalgpt}; RouteLLM, Hybrid LLM, AutoMix, MixLLM, and LLM Bandit learn to assign prompts to cheaper or stronger models based on predicted quality, preference, cost, or latency~\citep{ong2024routellm,ding2024hybridllm,madaan2024automix,mixllm2024,llmbandit2025}. This line is close in operational motivation because it also chooses among paid or costly inference options. The difference is the market interface: LLM routers typically operate as platform-side or service-layer selectors with published price cards and offline or semi-online evaluation over fixed model APIs. 402Pilot instead models a buyer that owns a wallet, observes payable endpoints through a protocol stack, makes an irreversible paid call to one provider, and updates from realized price and outcome feedback under reliability and price drift.

\paragraph{Mechanism design over provider markets.}
Reverse-auction mechanisms for contextual multi-armed bandits study a complementary provider-selection question: how a mechanism designer can elicit bids from strategic providers while maintaining truthful allocation and payment rules~\citep{patra2026reverseauction}. Our setting uses posted-price quote-and-pay endpoints rather than strategic bidding. The question is therefore not how to design a truthful market mechanism, but how a single autonomous buyer should spend over time once candidate payable services have already been exposed by the protocol layer.
