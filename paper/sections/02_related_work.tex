\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}

\paragraph{Programmable payment protocols and discovery.}
Programmable payment protocols expose the execution substrate for agent payments. x402 embeds per-request payment into HTTP, and its V2 release adds automatic discovery, dynamic payment recipients, extensible payment schemes, and dynamic service metadata, including pricing information~\citep{coinbase2025x402v2}. AP2 provides a payment-agnostic authorization framework for agent-led commerce~\citep{google2025ap2}; A2A--x402 brings x402-style cryptocurrency payments into the Agent-to-Agent protocol~\citep{a2ax402}; and A402 binds cryptocurrency payment to service execution through atomic service channels~\citep{a402}. A recent SoK organizes blockchain-based agent payments around discovery, authorization, execution, and accounting~\citep{sok2026agentpayments}. These systems make payable services discoverable and executable, but they do not decide how a wallet-bearing buyer should choose among those services over time. 402Pilot targets this missing layer: a buyer-side policy above payment execution that learns from realized prices, failures, and service outcomes.

\paragraph{Budgeted, contextual, and non-stationary bandits.}
Classical bandit work supplies the main algorithmic ingredients for our policy. Budgeted Thompson Sampling samples reward and cost posteriors under a finite budget~\citep{xia2015bts}, while bandits with knapsacks give a broader constrained-resource formulation~\citep{badanidiyuru2018bandits}. Contextual bandits estimate action value from task features, with Contextual Thompson Sampling and LinUCB as canonical examples~\citep{agrawal2013contextualts,li2010linucb}. Contextual bandits with knapsacks combine context with resource constraints~\citep{agrawal2016linearcbwk}. For non-stationary environments, discounted and sliding-window methods discount or window old observations~\citep{garivier2011discounted,trovo2020sliding,qi2023dsts}, and weighted linear bandits extend this idea to drifting contextual rewards~\citep{russac2019weighted}. These results cover important subsets of our design space, but they are not formulated as buyer-side policies inside a protocol-mediated quote--pay--observe loop. In our setting, the wallet drains, provider quality depends on the task, quality and price drift, and realized payment cost becomes feedback; the policy must handle all four together.

\paragraph{Cost-aware LLM routing and provider selection.}
A growing literature studies quality--cost tradeoffs in LLM routing. FrugalGPT uses cascades to reduce inference cost while preserving answer quality~\citep{chen2023frugalgpt}; RouteLLM, Hybrid LLM, AutoMix, MixLLM, and LLM Bandit learn to assign prompts to cheaper or stronger models based on predicted quality, preference, cost, or latency~\citep{ong2024routellm,ding2024hybridllm,madaan2024automix,mixllm2024,llmbandit2025}. This line is close in operational motivation because it also studies how an automated system should spend money across heterogeneous inference options. However, its decision object is usually a model call inside a known serving stack: prices are published, candidates are fixed, and the router mainly optimizes prompt-to-model assignment. 402Pilot shifts the unit of decision to wallet-constrained endpoint choice in a programmable-payment market. Market mechanisms such as reverse auctions address a different boundary: \citet{patra2026reverseauction} study incentive-compatible allocation and transfers under strategic LLM-provider bidding, whereas 402Pilot assumes posted-price quote-and-pay endpoints and focuses on how a single autonomous buyer should spend over time.
