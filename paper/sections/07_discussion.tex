\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discuss}

Agent micropayments are moving quickly from a protocol capability toward market infrastructure. x402 makes payment execution programmable, while complementary trust mechanisms for reputation, risk scoring, attestations, and escrow are beginning to emerge. Yet these mechanisms remain external to the buyer and are not yet uniformly trusted. 402Pilot therefore focuses on the buyer side: an agent must decide whether a quoted service is worth paying for using its own budget and observations. This decision layer complements future third-party trust platforms, much as human buyers may consult merchant ratings while still making their own purchasing decisions. We hope this framing encourages broader work toward micropayment agents that can act for users reliably, autonomously, and intelligently.

The main limitation is that our quantitative evaluation is deliberately controlled. 402Pilot-Bench uses frozen replay rather than live x402 calls so that provider outputs, seeds, costs, and non-stationary scenarios remain reproducible across policies. The local x402 witness in Appendix~\ref{appendix:x402-witness} validates that the payment-execution boundary can be instantiated on a protocol-compliant local stack, but it does not measure live settlement latency, facilitator failures, congestion, or provider drift in deployment. A second limitation is market coverage: the benchmark covers a finite set of task types, providers, and scripted market changes, so performance under broader tasks, more open-ended demand, unexpected price or reliability shifts, strategic providers, shared-wallet contention, and external trust signals remains future work. Finally, quality feedback is only as strong as the scorer or judge that supplies it, especially for open-ended tasks such as T3b; stronger verification, broader scenario sweeps, and tighter non-stationary theory are natural next steps. Appendix~\ref{appendix:sensitivity} reports sensitivity to $\gamma$; broader sweeps over $\nu$, $\alpha$, and $c_{\max}$, the bidirectional dual of the S3 promotion shock, and a full coupled non-stationary regret bound remain open.
