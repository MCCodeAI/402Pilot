\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discuss}

\paragraph{Discussion.}
The main implication of 402Pilot is that agent-native payment turns provider routing into a buyer-side online decision problem, not just a settlement problem. Atomic-payment protocols can make a chosen transaction safer, but they do not decide which provider a wallet-bearing agent should buy from as quality, reliability, realized price, and remaining budget change over time. 402Pilot isolates this pre-payment decision layer, and PA-DCT shows one way to turn both service outcomes and realized costs into learning signals. The evaluation should therefore be read less as a claim about one fixed provider menu and more as evidence that autonomous buyers need policies that jointly reason about quality, price, context, and budget pressure.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The main limitation is external validity. 402Pilot-Bench is a controlled frozen replay with calibrated providers and cached quality judgments, which makes paired comparisons reproducible but does not capture live x402 settlement effects, provider drift beyond the response buffer, strategic pricing, shared-wallet or multi-agent contention, or richer task/provider context. The open-ended T3b bucket is also evaluator-conditioned: fluent-but-wrong adversarial answers can sometimes pass the judge, so those results should not be read as absolute truthfulness detection. On the theory side, our clean regret statement is stationary and no-discount; the discounted result explains posterior adaptation after observations, not a full coupled regret theorem for every non-stationary wallet trajectory. Appendix~\ref{app:proof-limitations} gives the detailed proof scope. These boundaries point to the next steps: live deployment, stronger verification, richer market models, and tighter non-stationary theory.
