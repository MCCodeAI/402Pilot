\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We formalized \emph{agent-native bandits}: budget-aware, non-stationary, contextual decision-making in programmable micropayment markets where the bandit is the autonomous buyer itself, not a human-operated routing service. We proposed \emph{402Pilot}, a protocol-agnostic decision layer with stable interfaces between agent decision-making and payment execution, instantiated above x402 but applicable to any quote-and-pay programmable payment protocol. We proposed \emph{PA-DCT}, a payment-aware Thompson-sampling rule that maintains discounted dual posteriors over both quality and cost per (provider, task-type) bucket; treating cost as a learned signal rather than a known scalar is the structural commitment that lets a single policy detect price shocks and quality shocks symmetrically.

On 402Pilot-Bench---a reproducible benchmark of $824$ tasks $\times$ $5$ providers $\times$ $3$ calibrated market scenarios with $30$ seeds and $10{,}000$ rounds per cell---PA-DCT outperforms five baselines under both non-stationary scenarios with paired-bootstrap significance at $p<10^{-3}$, and pays a small honest exploration cost in the stationary baseline. A four-component ablation shows that each PA-DCT ingredient's contribution is exposed on a different evaluation axis: payment-awareness on cumulative reward (uniformly necessary), discount on adaptation time (35\% recovery acceleration in S2 outage), contextual bucketing selectively when shock structure is task-type heterogeneous, and Thompson sampling on seed variance ($5$--$9\times$ reduction) and shock-detection reliability. No single classical metric reveals all four; this motivates the multi-metric evaluation framework we recommend for the regime.
